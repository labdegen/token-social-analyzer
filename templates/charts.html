Technical Indicators Glossary
üìä
Moving Averages

Simple Moving Average (SMA)

Average price over a specific period. 20 SMA, 50 SMA, 200 SMA are common.

Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

Gives more weight to recent prices. More responsive than SMA to price changes.

How to Use:

  • Price above MA = Bullish trend
  • Price below MA = Bearish trend
  • MA crossovers signal trend changes
‚ö°
RSI (Relative Strength Index)

What it measures:

Momentum oscillator (0-100) measuring speed and change of price movements.

Key Levels:

  • Above 70 = Overbought (potential sell)
  • Below 30 = Oversold (potential buy)
  • 50 = Neutral momentum

Divergence:

When price and RSI move in opposite directions, often signals reversal.

üåä
MACD

Components:

  • MACD Line: 12 EMA - 26 EMA
  • Signal Line: 9 EMA of MACD
  • Histogram: MACD - Signal Line

Signals:

  • MACD crosses above Signal = Buy
  • MACD crosses below Signal = Sell
  • Above zero = Bullish momentum

Volume Indicators

Volume

Number of shares/tokens traded. Confirms price movements and breakouts.

Volume Profile

Shows volume traded at each price level. Identifies support/resistance.

Key Rules:

  • High volume + price move = Strong signal
  • Low volume + price move = Weak signal
  • Volume leads price

Support & Resistance

Support:

Price level where buying interest is strong enough to stop decline.

Resistance:

Price level where selling pressure stops upward movement.

Key Concepts:

  • Previous highs become resistance
  • Previous lows become support
  • Broken support becomes resistance
  • Round numbers act as psychological levels

Candlestick Patterns

Bullish Patterns:

  • Hammer: Small body, long lower wick
  • Engulfing: Large green candle engulfs previous red
  • Morning Star: Three-candle reversal pattern

Bearish Patterns:

  • Shooting Star: Small body, long upper wick
  • Evening Star: Three-candle reversal at top
  • Dark Cloud: Red candle engulfs green

Pro Chart Reading Tips

Timeframe Analysis

Multiple Timeframe Rule:

Always check higher timeframes for overall trend direction.

Common Timeframes:

  • 1m, 5m: Scalping entries
  • 15m, 1h: Day trading
  • 4h, 1D: Swing trading
  • 1W, 1M: Long-term investing

Golden Rule: Trade with the higher timeframe trend.

Drawing Tools

Trend Lines:

Connect swing lows (uptrend) or swing highs (downtrend). Need at least 2 points.

Horizontal Lines:

Mark key support/resistance levels from previous price action.

Fibonacci Retracements:

Common retracement levels: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%

Channels:

Parallel lines connecting highs and lows to identify trading ranges.

Risk Management

Stop Loss Placement:

  • Below support (long positions)
  • Above resistance (short positions)
  • Beyond pattern invalidation level

Take Profit Levels:

  • Previous resistance/support
  • Fibonacci extensions
  • Pattern price targets

Risk-Reward Ratio:

Aim for minimum 1:2 risk-reward ratio. Risk $1 to make $2.
